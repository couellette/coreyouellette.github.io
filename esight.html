    • Making big data fit in the palm of your hand
    • Data visualization tool
      November 2014
    • Traditionally after conducting a research study your data export typically consisted of rows and rows of data that would need to be exported into programs like SPSS to even make sense of out.
    • I was tasked with designing an application to visualize that data in a manner that is could fit on your smartphone, but still have the power to run advanced algorithms without compromising usability to the end user.
Setting the stage
Filtering, Crosstabbing, Collector analysis, and Data mining are not the type of words you traditionally think of when it comes to viewing data on a smartphone and/or tablet - this was my task to achieve. I needed to rethink how we could analyze and visualize our data without over simplifying the results. Some pains when it came to this was how can a graph with over ten row options be displayed in a manner that still maintains legibility, and how can I display verbatim responses on a limited screen size? As you can see above I came up with solutions such as a user can chose the graph type they would like to see their information, and for verbatim responses I leverage an internal scrolling frame allowing an unlimited amount of response to be viewable in a fixed piece of real-estate.
Long-term gains

Getting this opportunity really opened my eyes on how much information you can store in such a small piece of real-estate. This project really pushed my responsive design skills and helped me get to the next level of understanding for a Mobile-First mindset.

My key responsibilities for this were:

    • Wireframing the UI for both mobile and desktop environment
    • Front-end development of platform for all devices
    • Custom responsive framework for application
