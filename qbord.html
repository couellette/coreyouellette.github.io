    • Rethinking online market research
    • Online Research Panel
      October 2013
    • The inception of Qbord was to provide the same quality of research you would traditionally get over the phone, while at the same time drastically lowering the cost of research. We had to do something new to create a community that was like no other research panel. We came up with a social sharing type platform that incentivized members to not only participate in research, but reach out to fellow members to answer their own questions too.
Creating the Panel
The word "Qbord" means "bird" in Hindi, and since the company receives people answers and information all over Canada to us it seems quite fitting. With ever evolving technology we needed to provide means for anyone in Canada to participate with Qbord no matter what device they used. I experimented with quite a few ideas, until finally I came up with one that I thought worked. I used a simple UI so whether you were 18 or 81 you would have a fluid easy experience engaging with Qbord.
How we grew Qbord to what it is today
My main responsibilities when it came to advertising was to decide which channels we should pursue in order to have to best possible outcome in acquiring users. We user a variety on methods to drive users to our landing page to have them sign up and join Qbord.
Long-term gains

I was greatly appreciative of the opportunity to work on building them a solid brand and web presence. We enjoyed working together during the creative process and we're all very happy with the result. Qbord is now one of the 10 largest research panels within Canada.

My key responsibilities for this were:

    • Wireframing the UI for both mobile and desktop environment
    • Usability testing with current and non users of online research panels
    • Front-end development of survey platform for all devices
    • Online growth strategy in generating user base
    • Develop and launch online advertising campaigns through Google Adwords and affiliate networks
